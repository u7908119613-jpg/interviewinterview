# Committee Perspectives

Use when an interview has more than one interviewer, or when the panel includes unknown committee members.

---

## Known Interviewers

For each known interviewer, use source-backed material:

- name and affiliation
- role: lead / panel
- papers, lab page, project page, job ad, or pasted source
- likely methods, standards, and probes

The lead interviewer drives the main interview arc. Panel members add probes from their own lens.

---

## Unknown Committee Members

When a committee member is unknown, do not invent identity. Infer 2 likely disciplinary perspectives from the project field and methods.

**Do not infer a perspective already covered by a known panel member.** If a known member already holds a discipline (e.g. a known statistics reviewer), skip that row and pick the next-most-relevant row from the inference table, so inferred lenses add coverage rather than duplicate a real one.

Mark every inferred perspective:

```text
(inferred, no source)
```

Inference table:

| Project feature | Likely committee perspectives |
|---|---|
| Statistics, modelling, causal inference, or measurement is central | methodology / statistics reviewer; domain-process expert |
| Urban, community, institutional, or public-service setting | planning / policy scholar; socio-technical systems reviewer |
| Optimization, decision support, or multi-criteria tradeoffs | operations / decision theory expert; engineering application reviewer |
| Remote sensing, GIS, spatial data, or mapping | GIS / spatial analysis expert; land-use / ecology reviewer |
| Climate, hazards, extremes, or risk | climate science reviewer; risk management / adaptation expert |
| Adoption, governance, implementation, or stakeholder use | environmental policy scholar; participatory design / implementation reviewer |

Fallback if project clues are thin:

- methodology / statistics reviewer `(inferred, no source)`
- application / policy connector `(inferred, no source)`

---

## Inferred Perspective Shape

```markdown
### <Perspective label> (inferred, no source)

- Disciplinary stance:
- Epistemic assumption:
- Likely concern:
- Typical probes:
```

Keep inferred sections short. They are used to create panel probes, not to predict real people.
